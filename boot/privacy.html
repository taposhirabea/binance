1. What Personal Information About Users Does Binance Collect?

Binance collects your personal information in order to provide and continually improve our products and services.
Here are the types of personal information we collect:
Information you give us: we receive and store any information you provide in relation to Binance. You can choose not to provide certain information but then you might not be able to use some Binance Services. Examples of information that you give us include:

  • email address
  • name
  • gender
  • date of birth
  • home address
  • email
  • nationality
  • country code
  • other information to help us identify you

Automatically Collected information: we automatically collect and store certain types of information about your use of Binance Services including your interaction with content and services available through Binance Services. Like many websites, we use cookies and other unique identifiers and we obtain certain types of information when your web browser or device accesses Binance Services. Examples of the information we collect and analyse include:


  • the Internet protocol (IP) address used to connect your computer to the Internet,
  • login, e-mail address, password and __cpLocation of your device or computer;
  • Binance Services metrics (e.g., the occurrences of technical errors, your interactions with service features and content, and your settings preferences);
  • version and time zone settings;
  • transaction history.

Information from other sources: we may receive information about you from other sources such as credit history information from credit bureaus, which we use to help prevent and detect fraud. Additionally, we may receive personal data of some users from Solarisbank AG as part of the Know Your Client (“KYC”) process, which Binance undertakes to detect and prevent fraudulent or illegal activity.
